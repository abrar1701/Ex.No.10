# Ex.No.10
Content Creation (Reports, Articles, Case Studies, etc.) Using Prompt Patterns

## Date: 11-11-2025
## Reg. No.212223040111

## Aim:
To demonstrate how various prompting techniques (query decomposition, decision-making, semantic filtering, etc.) can be employed to create content such as reports, articles, case studies, or creative works like comic books, using ChatGPT or similar models. The objective is to highlight how different prompt structures affect the content's quality, coherence, and structure.

## Procedure:
1.	Introduction to Prompt Patterns: Begin by understanding the following prompt patterns:
    -	Query Decomposition: Breaking down complex queries into smaller, actionable parts.
    -	Decision Making: Asking the model to choose between options or directions.
    -	Answer Engineering: Refining outputs by giving detailed instructions on how to structure or format the answer.
    -	Fact Check List: Ensuring the content is factually accurate, especially for reports or case studies.
    -	Tail Generation: Extending the content logically to create depth in storytelling or analysis.
    -	Menu Actions: Presenting multiple action choices and guiding the AI to select one.
    -	Semantic Filter: Applying filters to control the tone, style, and accuracy of the content.
  2.	Choosing the Content Type: Decide on the type of content you want to create. This could be:
      -	Reports (e.g., industry analysis, sustainability reports, etc.)
      -	Case Studies (e.g., business solutions, technological innovations)
      -	Articles (e.g., opinion pieces, educational articles)
      -	Creative Content (e.g., comic book story, short stories, video scripts)

3.	For example, you could choose a business report on market trends, a case study on a successful startup, or even a story-based prompt like creating a fictional world for a comic.

4.	Creating the Prompts:
    -	Start with simple prompts to generate initial content.
    -	Gradually refine the prompts, moving toward more complex techniques like decision-making (asking the model to pick one of several options), tail generation (extending the narrative), and semantic filtering (adjusting style or tone).

5.	Generating and Refining Outputs: Use the model to generate initial drafts, and then refine the outputs using iterative adjustments:
      -	For a report, prompt the model with basic data, then ask for a deeper analysis and insights.
      -	For creative content, refine the initial story idea with specific details on characters, settings, or actions.

6.	Review and Evaluation: After generating content, evaluate the outputs for:

    -	Coherence: Is the generated content logical and structured?
    -	Creativity/Originality: Is the content engaging and fresh?  
    -	Accuracy: Is the content factually accurate (for reports and case studies)?
    -	Tone and Style: Does the content match the intended tone (formal, creative, educational)?

### Scenario 1: Creative Writing - Sci-Fi Short Story

The goal here is to use prompt patterns like Query Decomposition, Tail Generation, and Semantic Filter to create a coherent and engaging narrative.

### 1. Basic Prompt (First Draft):

Prompt Technique

Simple Prompt "Write a 300-word science fiction short story about an archaeologist who discovers an alien artifact that changes their perception of time."

Expected Outcome: A basic, unrefined story with a simple plot arc.

### Output:

Title: The Shard of Eternity

Dr. Lira Kael brushed away the Martian dust from the metallic object half-buried in crimson sand. Her pulse quickened—the artifact pulsed faintly, as if breathing. She’d spent years decoding ruins across the Valles Marineris, but nothing like this had ever been unearthed. It wasn’t rock or alloy. It hummed in tune with her heartbeat.

The moment her fingers touched its surface, the world fractured.

The excavation site stretched and folded like liquid glass. The crimson dunes bled into one another, and suddenly she was both standing here and a thousand years before. She saw the alien architects—tall, luminous beings—building vast citadels under twin suns. Then, in a blink, their cities crumbled to dust.

Time no longer obeyed sequence.

Lira saw herself as a child, stargazing from Earth’s orbiting academy. She saw her future self, old and frail, still chasing the truth of the universe’s origins. The visions spiraled—centuries collapsed into seconds. She understood time not as a river, but as a sphere: every moment existing all at once, waiting to be touched.

When the hallucinations subsided, the artifact hovered before her, spinning gently. A voice—or perhaps a memory—whispered in her mind:
"We built this to remember. Not to escape time, but to see it whole."

Her team’s shouts reached her ears. “Dr. Kael! Are you all right?”

She turned to them, but they seemed to move at glacial speed, their words dragging like echoes underwater. She realized she now moved outside their flow of time.

In her palm, the shard dimmed, then sank into her skin, leaving a faint golden mark.

As she stared at the Martian horizon, she no longer wondered what came next.
She could see all of it.

### 2. Refined Prompts (Detailed and Structured)

Now we introduce complexity to enhance the plot, character, and setting.

| **Prompt Technique**    | **Prompt Structure**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Query Decomposition** | """Part 1: Describe the alien artifact and the setting where the archaeologist, Dr. Elara Vance, finds it. Part 2: Detail the immediate effect the artifact has on Elara's sensory perception of time. Part 3: Write the closing paragraph where Elara realizes the artifact is not changing her perception, but is revealing the true nature of time.""" |
| **Tail Generation**     | """Take the story from the previous step and extend the ending. Dr. Vance must now decide if she should reveal the artifact to the world or keep the horrifying truth of 'true time' a secret. Write a new final paragraph detailing her decision."""                                                                                                     |
| **Semantic Filter**     | """Rewrite the entire story. The tone must be dark, paranoid, and claustrophobic. The style should mimic the concise, descriptive language of H.P. Lovecraft."""                                                                                                                                                                                          |
### Output:

Title: The Shard Beyond Hours

Part 1: The Discovery
The wind screamed across the Martian plateau, carving red scars into the desolation. Beneath the light of a dim and swollen sun, Dr. Elara Vance knelt at the lip of a collapsed tunnel once belonging to an ancient civilization unknown to humankind. Her hands trembled as her brush struck something metallic—smooth, curved, impossibly cold. She unearthed a shard no larger than her palm, blacker than vacuum, pulsing faintly as if it drew breath from the void itself. It emitted no sound, yet the silence around it grew thicker, pressing against her eardrums like deep water.

Part 2: The Fracturing of Perception
The instant her skin met its surface, Elara’s vision convulsed. The cave bent inward, its walls rippling like liquid mercury. Time slowed until her heartbeat became a thunderous, glacial pulse. Then, with a grotesque suddenness, everything accelerated—the dust fell upward, her reflection aged and decayed, and she watched the rise and collapse of civilizations in the span of a breath. The air around her no longer carried sound but memory, and she felt centuries pass through her like fever. The concept of “before” and “after” dissolved, replaced by an endless simultaneity that devoured her sense of self.

Part 3: Revelation of True Time
When the visions subsided, Elara found herself alone amid the whispering sands. The artifact hovered an inch above her palm, its pulse synchronized with her own. In that rhythm, she understood the terrible truth—the shard had not distorted time. It had shown her time’s true face: infinite, directionless, existing all at once like an organism whose heartbeat she could now hear. The illusion of chronology was a human frailty—a comfort for minds too fragile to endure eternity’s total exposure.

Final Decision
Back aboard her dimly lit outpost, Elara stared at the shard sealed behind glass. Reports would demand submission, data, proof. Yet as she watched the walls breathe with unseen centuries, she felt the presence of the artifact whisper in the recesses of her mind, urging silence. To reveal it would be to unmake human sanity, to peel away the last defense against the eternal chaos beneath existence. With trembling fingers, she deleted her findings, sealed the vault, and whispered to the dark, “Let them keep their illusion of time.”

Outside, Mars turned slowly beneath an ancient, indifferent sun—forever, all at once.
